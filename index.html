Molex Foundation Africa

Giving Back to Society

Molex Foundation Africa has been working diligently to support children and young people, especially the vulnerable and marginalised, to exercise their right and create equal platforms to pursue their dreams and potentials through mentoring and coaching. This is gradually improving the lives of many children in most deprived and disadvantaged communities in Ghana.

Additionally, the Foundation seeks to mobilize funds through grants and aids to reach a larger population with its activities. We also develop strategies to influence national policies, and engage other conventional influencers.

Our Priorities

To mentor, coach and guide children and young people to enable them to decide, learn, lead, and thrive.

To make sure that the girl-child has equal access and opportunity to quality and inclusive education.

To create a safe environment for children and women against all forms of violence, gender discrimination, and climate change catastrophe.

Molex Foundation Africa Mentorship and Coaching section for the Students of Yamoransa Meth. Basic Sch. As part of its vision to support school children in Africa through coaching and mentoring, the Foundation organized Career Guidance program for the Yamoransa Methodist Basic School students on 12th November, 2021 from 11:30am to 1:45pm at their school Assembly Centre. The one-day mentorship under the theme, “Setting academic goals and achieving them” was aimed at empowering the school children in various areas of their lives including personal development, academic excellence, personal hygiene, and student leadership. The main speaker, Dr. Patrick Essien who is a lecturer at University of Cape Coast and doubles as the CEO of the Molex Foundation, inspired the students by stating that they are the future leaders of the society so they have their books as their companions. He used his story of coming from a poor ground but he managed to study to this level as an instance. Dr. Essien ended the session by assuring them "this interaction will not end here." Molex Foundation Africa held a mentorship seminar on Career Development for students of Biriwa Methodist "B" Basic School on Day/Month/Year, at their school premises.
Ms. Charlotte Owusu-Ansah began her talk by asking a number of students to mention the work they would want to do in future. In an exciting mood, some gave responses as Nurse, Engineer, Police, Lawyer and Accountant, among others. He emphasized how equal and important each profession is in our contemporary society.
To achieve one's goal in career pathway, Ms. Charlotte indicated that one needs to have a mentor or coach who will from time-to-time guide him or her along the career ladder. She further advised the students to take their studies seriously because through that they will realize their dreams.
Ms. Owusu-Ansah concluded with an outline of tips on how the students can make most of their youthful life a success.

Molex Foundation Africa held a mentorship seminar on Career Development for students of Biriwa Methodist "B" Basic School on 18th March, 2022, at their school premises. Biriwa is a fishing community in central region where education is nothing to take home about and teenage pregnancy rate is really high. One of the speakers, Ms. Ivy Beauty Amantey, the Director of department of Children, central region, began her talk by asking a number of students to mention the work they would want to do in future. In an exciting mood, some gave responses as Nurse, Engineer, Police, Lawyer, Accountant, among others. He emphasized how equal and important each profession is in the society of mankind.
She went on to say that, to achieve one's goal in career pathway, one needs to have a mentor or coach who will from time-to-time guide him or her along the career ladder. She further advised the students to take their studies seriously. Ms. Ivy Beauty Amantey concluded with an outline of tips on how the students can make most of their youthful life fruitful. This was followed by question-and-answer section.
